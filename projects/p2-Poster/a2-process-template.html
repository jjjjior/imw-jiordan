2024 NBA All-Star Weekend Poster

Content Choice

When we were given the option to choose our own content, I wanted to choose something I had interest in. This led to the decision to make my poster about the 2024 NBA All-Star Weekend.

Interactive Ideas - Scrolling

One piece of interaction that I wanted to incorporate was scrolling text. This came from the https://alumniball.strelka.com/ but also related to the concept of a moving headline. This moving headline reminded me of stocks at the bottom of the CP24 News channel when it shows the sports teams. It also reminded me of a shooting star, similar to the one shown above in the 2024 NBA All Star logo.

Interactive Ideas - Pop-Up Ad

When it came to the Call-To-Action, I wanted to create something as a combination of a pop up advertisement and a header website advertisement. I never wanted it to leave the screen while also adding more attention by adding effects such as makin it beep.

Design Inspiration - Typography

When it came to typography, I wanted to try using a combination of different font classes like handwritten display, sans serif, and a serif display. These designs by Bailer Mincer are some pieces that I referenced but I also wanted to try mixing two different font classes in one word.

Design Inspiration - Homage

Since every NBA All-Star weekend is hosted in a different city, the branding is normally centered around the home NBA Team. In this case, the NBA team hosting all-star weekend are the Indiana Pacers. One design element that I would like to include is a checkered stripe to represent the race car scene in Indianapolis, e.g. the Indy 500. I also payed homage to the team by using their colour scheme of navy blue and gold. This colour scheme and checkered pattern are shown in this jersey from years prior.

Typography Variations

I tested out different headers that had different combinations of type. I also tested to see what words with different font classifications would look like.

Application

After deciding on a header display, I was able to add on the other elements of my poster such as a small description, events, and call-to-action.

Code Application and Addition of Interactivity

Once I got my layout figured out in figma, I was able to translate it in code to make a website. I had most of my problems within the use of different displays and especially the scrolling headers but I was soon able to figure it out. At first, I didn't have the players showcased through a scrolling element but I wanted to include it to create some importance to the all-star players. Some animations I added were scrolling headers, glows on yellow headers, and bounce animations on words to represent a basketball bouncing. The glow on yellow headers were inspired by big, bright lights as you see on huge neon signs. I also added the blinking animation on the call to action to by constantly lowering opacity. With the all-star name on the bottom, the west all stars came from the west (left) and the east all stars came from the east (right). This also felt like a race or duel when heading in each others directions due to the racing checkered stripe in the middle.
